Requirements that Guided Our Design of Driving Logic for AutoNav:
The main requirements were staying within the lane and not hitting cones.  Our MPC planner defines cost for touching and 
coming too close to the lane lines, cones, or other obstacles, and these requirements heavily guided our design because 
failing one of these requirements would mean that our AutoNav run is invalid.

Driving Logic for AutoNav:
For AutoNav, we use a MPC planner in 2 different modes, lane and GPS planning.  In lane planning, we sample points along 
a lane centerline as waypoints, and we use MPC to get to the next waypoint while minimizing cost by avoiding obstacles.  When 
we approach a GPS waypoint, we switch to GPS mode, and then we add waypoints between the GPS waypoints that form the pair, and 
again use MPC to travel along these waypoints.  The MPC creates trajectories which the car then follows, and progress is shown 
by passing these generated waypoints and moving along the course.  There is no special logic for dealing with complex 
obstacles, we rely on the MPC's planning to make good decisions.  To aid the MPC, we have a cost field that defines cost 
at obstacles, and also defines cost near obstacles.
