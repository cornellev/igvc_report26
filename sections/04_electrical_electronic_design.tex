\section{Electrical/Electronic Design}

For our electical design, we made the decision to use a 24V system, which provides a good balance between power and safety.
The main components of our electrical system include a 24V battery, motor controllers, sensors, a central motor controller logic unit, and a Jetson Orin Nano for processing sensor data and controlling the vehicle.
The 24V battery provides sufficient power for our motors while maintaining a manageable voltage level for safety.
We used the BLD-510C motor controllers for our BLDC motors, which offer precise control and are compatible with our chosen battery voltage.
These are controlled by our central motor controller unit via PWM signals, which allows for efficient and responsive motor control.
These are generated by a Raspberry Pi Pico, which also interprets the hall sensor data from the motors to figure out the RPM.
Additionally, for steering, we use a DMT542T stepper motor controller, which provides accurate control for our steering mechanism alongside an angle encoder for feedback.
The Jetson Orin Nano serves as the brain of our vehicle, sending commands to the motor controllers and recieving back wheels speeds and steering angle.
This is connected via ethernet to a Ethernet switch. The switch is connected to a travel router, our LiDAR, and FixedPosition VRTK-2 unit.
All the power is distributed after our custom isolation board, which can be toggled with the emergency e-stop unit and a physical switch. The isolation board also provides a single point of failure for the power distribution, which allows us to easily cut power to the entire system in case of an emergency.

The power distribution system is designed to handle the maximum current draw of all components, with a total capacity of approximately 30A. The battery can provide power for about an hour of run time under typical operating conditions.
